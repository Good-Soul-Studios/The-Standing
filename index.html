The Collapse

It wasn’t war, plague, or famine. It was something else. Something no one understood. They didn’t arrive with fire or violence. They just... appeared. Still. Silent. Watching.

At first, people laughed. Called it mass hysteria. Y2K panic. But the sightings spread. Reports flooded in. Streets emptied. Cities fell quiet.

And then They started standing in groups. Not moving. Not speaking. Just waiting.

You Are Cole

You don’t remember who you were. The world doesn’t care. Your memories are broken—shattered by exposure to Them. Even shapes feel wrong now. Places you knew don’t look the same.

“Down the hill I see Them. They haven’t moved. Six days now. Still. Watching.”

The Standing

They cluster in abandoned fields. Frozen rooftops. Shattered cities. They don’t breathe. They don’t blink. They just exist.

Some say those forms are empty—shells left behind. That the real ones have moved on. Drifted. Spread. Watching from places we can’t see. Listening. Waiting for warmth.

You’re not a person to them. You’re a signal. A presence. A target.
